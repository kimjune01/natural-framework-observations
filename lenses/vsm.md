# Viable System Model — Inter-Stage Contracts

Source: Beer, *Brain of the Firm* (1972), *Heart of Enterprise* (1979).

## Roles

| Role | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Operations (S1)** | Primary activities that produce the system's output |
| **Coordination (S2)** | Manages interactions and conflicts between operational units |
| **Control (S3)** | Monitors and optimizes operations as a whole |
| **Intelligence (S4)** | Scans the environment, models the future, plans adaptation |
| **Policy (S5)** | Sets identity, values, and ultimate purpose |

## Contracts

### Three Axioms of Management

Beer formalized three axioms — hard equality constraints on variety flow:

1. **First Axiom:** The sum of horizontal variety disposed by n operational elements (S1) equals the sum of vertical variety disposed by the six vertical components of corporate cohesion.
2. **Second Axiom:** The variety disposed by S3 equals the variety disposed by S4.
3. **Third Axiom:** The variety disposed by S5 equals the residual variety generated by the Second Axiom's operation.

### Requisite Variety (Ashby's Law, applied throughout)

"Only variety can absorb variety." Managerial, operational, and environmental varieties must be designed to equate. Amplifiers multiply low-variety inputs; attenuators dampen high-variety inputs.

### Per-Transition Contracts

| Transition | Contract |
|-----------|---------|
| S1 → S2 | S2 must attenuate variety from S1 units that would otherwise create destructive interference (anti-oscillation damping) |
| S2 → S3 | S2 provides coordinated, stabilized operations so S3 can optimize across all S1 units |
| S3 ↔ S4 | The 3-4 homeostat: S3 provides accurate state of current operations; S4 provides strategic adaptations. Variety disposed must be equal (Second Axiom) |
| S4 → S5 | S5 monitors the 3-4 homeostat. S4's proposals must remain within S5's policy framework |
| S5 → all | S5 handles only residual variety from the 3-4 balance (Third Axiom). Provides identity and closure |

### Channel Capacity

"Channels for communicating variety must hold higher capacity than their transmissions in order to account for errors."

### Transduction

"Wherever information carried on a channel crosses a boundary, it undergoes transduction; the variety of the transducer must be at least equivalent to the variety of the channel."

### Algedonic Channel

Direct emergency signal from S1 to S5, bypassing hierarchy. Timeout-based: S1 gets first chance to correct; if unresolved, escalates to S3, then S5.

### Recursion

Every viable system contains viable subsystems. The same constraints apply fractally at every level.

## Contract Strength

**Strong.** Beer provides mathematical axioms (equality constraints on variety), not just descriptions. The variety equations are quantitative. Channel capacity and transduction requirements are formal. VSM may be the closest competitor to NF in contract rigor — but its contracts are about variety flow between management levels, not about data content at handoffs.
